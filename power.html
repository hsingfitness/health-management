What Is Power?

Power is the ability to produce force quickly. It combines strength and speed to create explosive movement.

From jumping and sprinting to martial arts and sports, power determines how efficiently your body can generate movement.

The Science of Power

Power depends on two main factors:

  • Force production (strength)
  • Movement velocity (speed)

Improving power requires training the nervous system to produce force rapidly while maintaining control.

Power Training Methods

Plyometric Training

Exercises such as jumps and bounds improve the ability to generate force quickly.

Explosive Strength Training

Fast movements with resistance develop the ability to apply strength at high speed.

Speed Training

Sprinting, acceleration drills, and reaction training improve movement velocity.

Dynamic Movement

Rotational movements, throws, and athletic drills improve whole-body coordination.

Power Exercises

  • Jump squats
  • Medicine ball throws
  • Kettlebell swings
  • Olympic lifting variations
  • Sprint intervals
  • Explosive push-ups

Benefits of Power Training

  • Increase athletic performance
  • Improve reaction speed
  • Enhance coordination
  • Develop explosive movement
  • Improve functional ability
  • Support healthy aging

Strength + Control = Power

True power does not come from strength alone. It requires a strong foundation, precise control, and the ability to express force at the right moment.

The Strength Control Power system follows a simple progression:

  1. Build Strength
  2. Develop Control
  3. Express Power
